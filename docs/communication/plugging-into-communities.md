# Plugging into Communities and Platforms

A lot of our reach comes from tapping into communities and platforms that already
exist. The goal is to get PyConZA news — the CFP, ticket sales, speaker
announcements, and so on — in front of the right people without having to build
that audience from scratch.

This page is a living reference. As we discover new communities and platforms,
add them here so the next person doesn't have to start from scratch.

## Conference listing platforms

These platforms aggregate conference and CFP information. Getting PyConZA listed
gives us free, targeted exposure to people who are actively looking for events to
attend or speak at.

- **[Python Deadlines (pythondeadlin.es)](https://pythondeadlin.es/)** — a
  community-maintained list of Python conference and CFP deadlines. Listings are
  managed via a GitHub repository, so we can submit a pull request to add or
  update PyConZA.

## Finding new groups to announce to

Communities come and go, so part of the comms job is actively searching for new
places to plug into. Some approaches:

- **Meetup.com** — search by topic (Python, Django, data science, tech) and by
  South African cities. Filter for groups that are still active (recent events).
- **University and college societies** — computer science and engineering student
  societies often run their own channels.
- **Slack / Discord communities** — many local tech communities live in chat
  platforms rather than on event sites.
- **LinkedIn and X/Twitter** — search hashtags and location-based tech
  communities; many organisers cross-post there.
- **Ask the network** — past attendees, speakers, and sponsors often know about
  niche communities we haven't reached yet.

When you find a new community, add it to the relevant section above with a contact
and a note on the best way to reach them.
